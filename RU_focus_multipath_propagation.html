🔍 What you're seeing:

Left Panel (Spatial View): Shows the physical signal arriving at the antenna array. The colored lines represent wavefronts traveling from the transmitter. Notice how the signal reaches different antennas at slightly different times due to the extra distance it must travel.

Right Panel (Phase View): Shows the actual received signal at each antenna as a sine wave. The horizontal offset between waves represents the phase difference. When the Angle of Arrival (AoA) is 0°, all antennas receive the signal in phase. As the angle changes, you'll see increasing phase shifts between antennas.

Key Insight: Phase difference Δφ = (2π × d × sin(θ)) / λ, where d is antenna spacing, θ is AoA, and λ is wavelength. This phase difference is what allows the array to determine the direction of arrival and perform beamforming!
