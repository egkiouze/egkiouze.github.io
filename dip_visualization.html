📖 Understanding the Delay Intensity Profile (DIP):

The Delay Intensity Profile describes how signal power is distributed across different propagation delays in a multipath channel. The exponential DIP is a common model where path power decays exponentially with delay:
P(τ) = P₀ · exp(-τ / τ_rms)
where τ_rms is the RMS delay spread, a key parameter that characterizes channel dispersion.

Left Panel (Continuous Time): Shows the idealized continuous power delay profile. The blue curve represents the exponential envelope, while red impulses show individual multipath components that arrive at different delays with powers following the exponential decay.

Right Panel (Discrete Time): Shows how the channel is represented in practical digital systems. The continuous channel is sampled at regular intervals (sample period T_s = 1/f_s). Multiple paths arriving within one sample period are combined into a single discrete "tap" (green bars). Orange markers show where individual paths contribute to each tap.

Key Concepts:
• RMS Delay Spread: Determines how quickly power decays with delay (smaller = faster decay)
• Sampling Rate: Higher rates provide better time resolution but more taps
• Tap Aggregation: Paths within one sample period combine coherently at each tap
• ISI (Inter-Symbol Interference): Multiple taps cause symbols to spread in time, requiring equalization
